notes from the designer

The default view is what a doctor sees when she clicks Memory between visits. She isn't here for a ritual; she's here to look something up. So the lead is the lectern — one italic line that takes a name (Mrs. Chen), a topic (q3 pricing), or a date (last tuesday). Geomi is the breathing dot inside it; the conversational way in is built into the input, not a chatbot panel.

Below the lectern is the library, tabbed by how a clinician actually thinks: recent first (because a real commonplace book falls open to the pages you just used), then people, topics, meetings. Each row is name in roman + a phrase of contents in italic + when. A small italic e beside a row means the user has co-authored that page through marginalia — principle 2 surfaced as a pen-mark, not a badge.

The morning ritual lives as one quiet line above the lectern. 3 marginalia from yesterday are still waiting for your initials. Click and the morning view opens; ignore it and the book is still yours. The ritual is a sub-mode, never the homepage.
