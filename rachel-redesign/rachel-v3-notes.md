# v3 redline notes — Rachel

Sean asked for one mockup this round, focused: the Memory landing page, the moment a doctor clicks "Memory" in the sidebar between visits. One screen. Three seconds to "I get it."

## What changed from v2

**Killed the gallery.** v2 was a six-surface mockup-index — useful for review, terrible as a landing. The new file is the thing itself: titlebar + 200px sidebar + one calm pane. Memory is a destination, not a tour.

**The masthead is a sentence.** *"the book, this morning."* No CAPS surface name, no version chip, no badge. Lowercase italic serif. Principle 1: the page tells you what it is in the same voice it speaks in.

**The morning re-read is the lede, full stop.** Three marginalia in serif prose, two replies each — *Yes, remember that* / *Not yet*. The Geomi dot is in the lede sentence, not a corner widget. This is the surface; everything else supports it. Principle 2 by composition, not by section heading.

**Open-a-page replaces every catalog.** One italic input line. "by name, or a date you want to revisit." No filters, no kinds, no schema chrome.

**Recent pages read like spines.** Name in roman, a phrase of what's inside in italic, time on the right. No paths. No counts.

## What the book metaphor unlocked

Once the unit is *page*, every list looks like a shelf and every entry has a phrase-of-contents instead of a filename. "Mrs. Chen — what we know, after sixteen visits" tells the doctor what's inside; `cortex/person/mrs-chen.md` tells her about our filesystem. The metaphor also licenses the single vellum hairline under the masthead and the 3px stripe down each marginalia card — diegetic, earned, not theme-park.

## What I'd push for in v4

1. **Voice replies inline.** A mic affordance on the marginalia, not a separate mode. The doctor should initial while washing her hands.
2. **The morning ritual ends.** When the three are confirmed, the stack collapses to one serif line — *"all caught up. the book is yours today."* No emoji, no celebration animation. Just quiet.
3. **A page open.** v3 only shows the landing. v4 should show what one page (Mrs. Chen) looks like when you click in — three to five paragraphs of prose, one "ask Geomi to update" affordance, no field labels.
