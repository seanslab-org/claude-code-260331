notes from the designer · v4.1

The year ribbon was the quietest piece of v4 — a decoration. Here it becomes the second instrument on the page. Click a month and the galaxy doesn't reorganize; it just recedes, leaving that month's notes lit. The companion strip beneath surfaces those meetings as one-line italic prose — no fields, no chips. Drag the pin leftward and future notes simply aren't there yet, the galaxy contracts to that moment, the label whispers as of nov 14. Release and time snaps forward.

The strip sits above the ribbon (option a), full-width and horizontal-scrolling, rather than as a floating card. A floating card covers the galaxy you just spotlit — the whole point of the gesture is to keep your eyes on the dots. The strip is calmer, doesn't anchor, and reads like a memoir caption to the picture above it.

This ribbon is groundwork for the people lens coming in Phase 3: the same spotlight gesture, but pinned to a person rather than a month — k. liu's notes glow, everything else recedes, the strip becomes what k. liu has been working on, what they committed to, who needs care. Time and people share the same recall ritual; we just changed the lens.
