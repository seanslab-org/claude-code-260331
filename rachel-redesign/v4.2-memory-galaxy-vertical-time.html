notes from the designer · v4.2

The horizontal ribbon was always pretending to be a calendar; turning it vertical lets it be one. The galaxy is now sky — uninterrupted — and the time column on the right is the calendar pinned to its margin, always visible, scoped from the finest grain (this week) down to the coarsest (all time). One column of italic-serif rows, no chrome, no dropdown — the entire time horizon is legible at a glance.

The ask bar moves to the bottom and adopts the Notes-view bottom-bar shape: pill, breathing Geomi on the left, scope pill in the middle, ⌘K hint on the right. Memory and Notes now share the same primary gesture surface; the user only learns one motion. Click anywhere in the time column to spotlight; click the breadcrumb's × or press esc to release. Spotlight is the only gesture on the landing — drag-pin rewind belongs to per-engram time-travel and has been dropped from here.

What's next is the people lens for v5. The right column gets a twin underneath it: people — k. liu, d. park, mrs. chen — same affordance, same spotlight, but lit by who-you-met-with rather than when. Time and people are two readings of the same galaxy.

— Rachel · 4 May 2026 · click any row · esc to release
