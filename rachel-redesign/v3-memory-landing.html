notes from the designer

The masthead is a sentence, not a header. The book, this morning. No surface name in CAPS, no badge, no "Memory v8" — the user already clicked Memory in the sidebar. Principle 1: the title is what the page is, in plain words.

The lede is the morning re-read because principle 2 says it has to be. Three marginalia in serif prose, two replies each — Yes, remember that is the encoding event. No field paths, no diff cards, no model name. Geomi is the breathing dot, present in the sentence, not boxed into a panel.

"Open a page" is one italic line. Recent pages read like spines on a shelf — name, then a phrase of what's inside, never a path. The book metaphor stays felt, not literal: a single vellum hairline under the masthead and a 3px stripe down the left edge of each marginalia card. That is all the skeuomorphism the page earns.
