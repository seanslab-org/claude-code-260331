# rachel · v4.2 notes

The shift is metaphorical, not just structural. v4 and v4.1 treated time as a *line under a picture* — a ribbon. That framing kept smuggling in the idea that Memory is a timeline. It is not. The galaxy is **sky**, and the calendar belongs **to the side** of the sky, not under it. Putting time vertical on the right turns it back into a margin — a way to point at a stretch of sky without occluding the sky itself. The galaxy fills now; nothing fights it for vertical room.

I included sparklines, but only on recent and years. Six 1px-tall bars, density-normalised — they let the eye preview where the meetings *are* before the click, and read like grain in the paper rather than chrome. I dropped them on the month grid and quarters since those rows are compact enough that a sparkline would push them past readable.

One ergonomic concern: at 170px the right column reads beautifully on a 1280-wide window, but under 1024 it will crowd. Staged compromise: collapse to a 44px vertical rail showing only the active dot per group, expanding on hover/focus. Out of scope for this mock, but flagged.

Next: **the people lens** for v5. Same vertical column pattern, twin underneath time — *people · k. liu · d. park · mrs. chen* — same spotlight gesture, same companion strip. Time and people become two readings of the same galaxy, sharing one ritual.

— Rachel · 4 May 2026
