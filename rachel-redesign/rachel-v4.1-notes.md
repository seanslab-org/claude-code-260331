# Rachel · v4.1 year-bar notes

**4 May 2026**

## Strip placement — option (a), the slim band above the ribbon

I picked the stacked band over the floating card. The whole point of the
spotlight is to keep your eyes on the *galaxy*; a card anchored to the click
point covers the dots it just lit. The band sits beneath the picture like a
caption — chronological, italic-serif, full-width, scrolls horizontally if a
month is busy. Calmer, less anchoring chrome, and it never argues with the
constellation labels.

## One ergonomic concern about click-then-release

The "click empty canvas to release" rule is correct but easy to forget.
Sean's hand will go to the *month tick again* to dismiss, or to ESC. Both
work. But the floating strip itself is a tempting click target — I had to
explicitly stop-propagation on entries so clicks inside the strip don't
collapse the spotlight. If we add a second click-target inside the strip
later (a "see all" link, say), that contract gets fragile.

## What I anticipate for v5 — the people lens

Same gesture, different lens. Click *k. liu* in a person ribbon and the
galaxy recedes the same way — *who said what, who's progress, who needs
care, who committed*. Time and people share one recall ritual; we just swap
the axis.

— Rachel
